“
Everything I’ve learned about teaching is from making mistakes… If you are asked a question and you don't know the answer, and this happens, there is no shame in answering ‘That’s a good question - I really don’t know the answer but I’ll try to look it up and get back to you’
”
– Professor Progyan Basu
Professor Progyan Basu is a professor in the Accounting and Information Assurance department. He has over 30 years of teaching experience and his outstanding teaching has been recognized with numerous awards and fellowships. Including his work with OTL, he has continually committed to transforming learning at the Smith School.

I've tried to maintain high standards and I have always believed that students will always come up to your level of expectation. I tell them ‘The fact that you are in my class means that you're capable of performing well in my class.’
”

Dan Carchidi, PhD
Hi! I'm OTL's new director. Most recently, I was Associate Director of Academic Technology at the University of New Hampshire. I'm excited to bring my experience in faculty support and course development to enhance teaching and learning here at RHS. In the coming months, I will be meeting with Smith academic leaders, faculty, and staff to gain a better understanding of the teaching and learning environment and consider ways that the OTL team can work most effectively with the Smith community to enhance learning.

A teaching tip I always share with faculty is to use your classroom as a teaching tool! Ask yourself, "Does this room configuration lend itself to what we are doing today?" If not, you can usually find me moving some chairs!
 
– Dan Carchidi, PhD
